Butterfly

  • Butterfly wings are transparent
  • Butterflies use their front feet to taste
  • There are almost 20,000 butterfly species

Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat

Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Pigeons

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  • Pigeons are reowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities

Teddy Bear

  • The word or name 'teddy bear' comes fromt he nickname of Theodore Roosevelt
  • There is a dedicated magazine to teddy bears, called 'Teddy Bear and Friends' that has about 40,000 subscribers
  • The world's largest teddy bear, that won this record in 2019, is the teddy from Mexico at 19.41m in length

Panda

  • Pandas go fro pink to white and black (or brown)
  • An adult panda can eat 12-38 kilos of bamboo per day
  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food

Flying-Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus
  • Most cicadas have red-orange eyes
